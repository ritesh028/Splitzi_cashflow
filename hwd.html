HOW WE DO
Feed the debts to the algorithm in the form of a directed graph (let's call it G)
Choose one of the directed graph G's non-visited edges, such as (u, v)
Run a maxflow algorithm (perhaps Dinic's maxflow algorithm, as it's one of the best implementations) with u as the source and v as the sink to calculate the maximum amount of money that may flow from u to v
Also, after eliminating the flow of debts between u and v, compute the Residual Graph (let's call it G'), which shows the extra potential flow of debts in the input graph
If the maximum flow between u and v (let's call it max-flow) is greater than zero, then add an edge (u, v) to the Residual Graph with the weight max-flow.
Return to Step 1 and input the Residual Graph G' to it
The Residual Graph G' created in the last iteration will be the one with the least number of edges once all of the edges have been visited (i.e. transactions)
